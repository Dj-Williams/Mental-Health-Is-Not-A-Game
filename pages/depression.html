What is Depression?

Depression is described as a mental state of persistent low-self worth/value/esteem, and is often paired with an extreme difficulty to enjoy the things in life that previously brought fufillment. Depression is different from general sadness or grieving the loss of a loved one, because the latter two are defined and clear causes as to why one would be in a mentally down state. Depression has a myriad of causes, which can vary from the biochemical to the psychosocial, and is generally marked with disproportionate melancholic responses in both the duration and intensity of the emotion.

How does it affect people?

According to the WHO (World Health Organization) 300 Million people suffer from depression and depressive symptoms globally.

16.2 Million adults in the US have experienced a major depressive episode in the past year, that roughly 6.7% of all adults in the country.

Depression is the most prevalent in the 18-25yr old demographic.

60% of children with depression don't receive the treatment they need and are likely to develop more severe symptoms over time.
